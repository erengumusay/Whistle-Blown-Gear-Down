Economic Progress and Female Athlete Representation

Research Question 3

Research Focus

This study will examine the patterns in the number of female athletes sent to the Olympics throughout time and how these changes correspond to the nations' economic progress, as defined by GDP.

The purpose is to determine how economic growth affects the progress of female athlete representation and if nations with greater GDP are more effective in achieving gender equality in sport.

Key Investigative Areas

  • Temporal trends in female athlete representation
  • Correlation between GDP and female athlete participation
  • Economic factors influencing gender equality in sports

Whistle Blown, Gear Down

The Impact of Istanbul's Big Three's Matches on Traffic

Introduction

This is a research to explore Beşiktaş, Fenerbahçe, and Galatasaray’s football matches’ effects on Istanbul traffic. The main goal is to analyze their daily, hourly and overall affect. We aim to discover key trends and attributes by analyzing time and day based traffic density data within a 50-square-kilometer area centered around each stadium. Our analysis will be conducted from 2 PM to 1 AM to observe match affects better by not getting too far away from their starting time. Ultimately, we hope to answer whether, as many believe, match days are causing significantly more traffic.
